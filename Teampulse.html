The Prototype

Nine precision sensors fused in real time by an ESP32-S3-WROOM-1, generating a risk score every 10 seconds with multi-modal alerts.

Risk Classification

Normal — Green BR <25 bpm · No wheeze · Clean air · AQI 1–2
Elevated Risk — Yellow BR 25–30 · Wheeze >0.3 · Pollution index >0.7
Attack Warning — Red BR >30 · Chest tightness · Wheeze+pollution combo

Processing Pipeline

01
Sensor Acquisition (~10 ms)

All 9 sensors read via I2C & SPI every 10 seconds. Audio buffer captured for FFT.

02
Signal Processing (~15 ms)

Peak detection for breathing rate. FFT band isolation 100–500 Hz for wheeze. WHO-normalised pollution index.

03
Risk Classification (~5 ms)

Threshold matrix combines all parameters into Green / Yellow / Red score.

04
Alert Generation (~5 ms)

LED, vibration motor, buzzer, BLE to mobile app. Future: NHSL cloud dashboard.

Data & Evidence

Research & Impact

Built on peer-reviewed epidemiological data, RespiraSense addresses a nationally disproportionate asthma burden driven by Colombo's severe air pollution crisis.

🌏
23.9%

Sri Lanka Adult Prevalence

Gunasekera et al. (BMC Public Health 2022), conducted across 7 provinces and 1,872 subjects — nearly 5× the global average of 4–5%.

🌫️
72–82

Colombo PM10 µg/m³

Over 4× WHO's guideline of 20 µg/m³. AQI regularly spikes above 111 (Unhealthy) during peak hours — vehicle emissions account for 70% of total pollution.

🏥
179K

Annual Hospitalisations

815.5 per 100,000 population (SL Ministry of Health). Estimated LKR 5 billion in ER costs annually; 1,000 deaths in government hospitals per year.

🌙
50%

Nocturnal Attack Deaths

Over 50% of asthma deaths occur between midnight and 8 AM. Cortisol drops reduce airway protection; 75% of patients wake with symptoms at least weekly.
